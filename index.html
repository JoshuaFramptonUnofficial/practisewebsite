What the evidence says

Evening showers can support sleep by aiding thermoregulation when timed about 90 minutes before bedtime, and help remove daytime grime and allergens that accumulate on skin and hair.

  • Warm showers 1–2 hours before bed can assist sleep onset by facilitating a gentle body‑temperature drop.
  • Rinsing at day’s end removes sweat, oils, and pollutants accumulated across daily activities, supporting cleanliness.
  • Colds are caused by viruses, not by shower timing or wet hair; basic hygiene and exposure reduction remain key.

Practical recommendations

Keep showers warm, not hot, for 5–10 minutes, moisturize soon after, and time the routine to allow wind‑down before sleep.

  • Short, warm showers protect skin barrier and comfort.
  • Consider a brief cool rinse if preferred, but avoid over‑cooling late at night.
  • Maintain clean bedding to reduce allergen buildup and odors.

Notes on claims

Avoid implying institutional endorsements or using protected logos; use neutral visuals and cite sources when communicating health considerations.

  • Focus on accurate, non‑misleading statements about cleanliness and sleep hygiene.
  • Refrain from attributing colds to shower timing, and prioritize evidence‑based guidance.
